WHO Covid-19 Analysis

WHO Covid-19 Dashboard

COVID-19, also known as coronavirus disease 2019, is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It emerged in December 2019 and quickly spread worldwide, leading to a global pandemic. The virus primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks

This analysis therefore seeks to obtain information about the Covid-19 Situation according to WHO.

DATA SOURCE: WHO website

Tools Used: Tableau

Objective: To understand the occurrence, trend and gravity of the COVID-19 pandemic.

DATA OVERVIEW

The data contained a total of 12 columns and 237 rows . The colums hold information about:

Country

Who Region

-Cases-Culmunative Total

- Case per 100000 of Population

- Cases - newly Reported in last 7 days

- Cases - newly reported last 7 days per 100000 of population

- Cases in last 1 hour

-Deaths-Culmunative Total

- Deaths per 100000 of Population

- Deaths - newly Reported last 7 days

- Deaths - newly reported last 7 days per 100000 of population

- Deaths in last 1 hour

DATA CLEANING AND ANALYSIS

The Data Set did not require any Cleaning

DATA VISUALIZATION

I uploaded the dataset to tableau desktop which I used for Analysis and visualization .

The(KPIs) were calculated using the Tableau measures Values. The formulars used are as follows:

-Cases=sum(-Cases-Culmunative)

-Last 7 days = sum(cases - last 7 days)

-per 100000 pop= sum(cases per 100000 of population)

.

FINDINGS

-

United States (103,436,829) and China(99,320,286) are the Countries with the highest Number of cases

- united State and Brazil Hold the record of the highest Number of deaths.

-

Europe and the Americas Are the WHO Regions with the most cases per 100000 of population

- African, Eastern Mediterarnean and South East Asian countries are the countries hit the least by the pandemic
